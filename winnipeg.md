# Winnipeg: A City Overview

Winnipeg is the capital city of Manitoba and one of the most culturally diverse and historically significant cities in Canada. Known as the “Gateway to the West,” Winnipeg blends Indigenous heritage, multicultural communities, and modern urban development.

---

## 📍 Location
Winnipeg is located at the geographic center of North America, where the Red River and Assiniboine River meet. This area is known as **The Forks**, one of the city’s most popular public spaces.

## 🏛 Major Attractions

### **The Forks**
A historic meeting place transformed into a modern market, riverside park, and cultural hub.

### **Canadian Museum for Human Rights**
A globally recognized museum dedicated to human rights education, featuring iconic architecture.

### **Assiniboine Park & Zoo**
A large urban park with gardens, trails, and the famous *Journey to Churchill* polar bear exhibit.

### **Exchange District**
A National Historic Site known for:
- Early 20th‑century architecture  
- Art galleries  
- Cafés  
- Film production  

---

## 🌦 Climate
Winnipeg experiences four distinct seasons:
- Warm summers  
- Colorful autumns  
- Cold but bright winters  
- Refreshing spring weather
